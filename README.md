# Global GDP Data Pipeline & Analysis

An ETL and exploratory data analysis project that merges two Kaggle GDP datasets into a unified 66-year longitudinal time series (1960–2025) spanning 196 countries, then performs structured economic analysis and visualization.

---

## What this project does

Two separate CSV datasets — one covering 1960–2020, one covering 2020–2025 — are cleaned, normalized to a common unit (billions USD), and merged into a single long-format dataset. That dataset is then explored across seven analytical dimensions: data quality, statistical distributions, global trends, growth rates, wealth concentration, country-level trajectories, and economic shock analysis.

Key findings the analysis surfaces:
- World GDP grew roughly **99× from 1960 to 2024** ($1.1T → $110.5T)
- GDP distribution is highly right-skewed — a handful of large economies dominate
- The Gini coefficient of GDP across countries has risen over time, indicating increasing concentration
- China's share of world GDP grew from near zero in 1960 to rival the United States by the 2020s
- The 2008–09 GFC and COVID-19 (2020) are visible as the two sharpest global contractions in the record

## Data sources

| Dataset | Years | Source |
|---------|-------|--------|
| World GDP (wide format, raw USD) | 1960–2020 | [Ozgur Cem Tas — Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/zgrcemta/world-gdpgdp-gdp-per-capita-and-annual-growths) |

### Merged dataset schema (`full_gdp_data_1960_2020.csv`)

| Column | Type | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| `Country` | string | Country name (196 unique) |
| `Year` | int64 | Year (1960–2025) |
| `GDP` | float64 | GDP in billions of USD (current prices) |

~11,376 rows. GDP ranges from $0.01B to $30,507B (United States, 2025). **1,990 null values (~17%) are left as-is** — they represent genuine absence of statistical reporting (small island nations, post-colonial and post-Soviet states in early decades) and are not imputed to avoid distorting aggregate calculations.

## Notebook 01 — Preprocessing (ETL)

**Input:** `data/1960-2020.csv`, `data/2020-2025.csv`  
**Output:** `data/full_gdp_data_1960_2020.csv`

Steps:
1. Load both raw CSVs and inspect shape, dtypes, and coverage
2. Reshape both from wide format (one column per year) to long format (`Country`, `Year`, `GDP`)
3. Normalize units — the 1960 dataset uses raw USD; the 2020 dataset uses millions USD. Both are converted to **billions USD** and rounded to 2 decimal places
4. Drop the spurious trailing column (`Unnamed: 65`) from the 1960 dataset
5. Remove the duplicate 2020 row from the 1960 dataset (2020 is authoritative from the newer source)
6. Filter the 1960 dataset to only the 196 countries present in the 2020 dataset
7. Validate: zero negative GDPs, zero duplicate Country–Year pairs
8. Concatenate and export

## Notebook 02 — Exploratory Data Analysis

**Input:** `data/full_gdp_data_1960_2020.csv`  
**Visualizations:** static (matplotlib / seaborn) + interactive (plotly)

### Section 1 — Data Quality & Missing Values
- Bar chart: top 20 countries by number of missing GDP years
- Line chart: missing values per year, annotated with the USSR dissolution (1991) as a structural break
- Heatmap: all 196 countries × all 66 years, color-coded by presence/absence of data

### Section 2 — Summary Statistics & Distribution
- Dataset overview: row count, country count, null count, descriptive stats
- World GDP totals for 1960 and 2024 with growth multiple
- GDP histograms for 2024 on linear and log₁₀ scale (illustrating extreme right skew)
- Box plots of GDP distribution grouped by decade on log scale

### Section 3 — Global Temporal Trends
- World GDP area chart (1960–2025) annotated with major economic shocks: 1973 Oil Crisis, 1980 Debt Crisis, 1997 Asian Crisis, 2008 GFC, 2020 COVID-19
- Interactive log-scale line chart showing all 196 countries simultaneously
- Year-over-year global GDP growth rate bar chart, highlighting 2009 and 2020 contractions

### Section 4 — Growth Analysis
- CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) rankings — top 15 fastest-growing and bottom 15 slowest/shrinking economies over their full data history
- Median country GDP growth rate by decade
- Scatter plot: latest GDP size vs growth volatility (standard deviation of annual % change), with major economies labelled

### Section 5 — Concentration & Inequality
- Stacked area chart: share of world GDP over time for the US, China, Germany, Japan, and the rest of the world
- G2 focus: combined US + China share of world GDP
- Gini coefficient of GDP across countries by year — tracking whether economic output has become more or less concentrated

### Section 6 — Notable Country Trajectories
- **China vs United States** — log-scale comparison annotated with Deng Xiaoping's Reform & Opening (1978), WTO accession (2001), and the GFC (2008)
- **Asian Tigers** — South Korea, Singapore, Hong Kong
- **Japan's Lost Decades** — annotated with the Plaza Accord (1985) and asset bubble collapse (1991)
- **Post-Soviet transitions** — Russia, Ukraine, Kazakhstan from 1988, annotated with USSR dissolution (1991) and Russian default (1998)
- **Latin America** — Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, annotated with the 1982 Debt Crisis and Argentina's 2001 default

### Section 7 — Economic Shocks Deep Dive
- Top 20 countries most affected by the 2008–09 GFC (% GDP change 2008→2009)
- Scatter plot: COVID-19 shock depth (2019→2020 % change) vs recovery speed (2020→2022 % change), with major economies labelled
